# Play Fast Notes — Master Vocabulary

*Sources: Dr. Molly Gebrian "The Amazing List of Practice Techniques" (University of Arizona) + Ralph Skiano*

---

## SESSION TYPES

- **Massed Practice** — Focused repetition of a single passage. Essential in early learning stages. Builds correct neural pathways through concentrated repetition.
- **Interleaved Practice** — Rotating randomly between several passages every few repetitions. More effective as a performance approaches. Tracks success across passages.
- **Technical Focus** — Dedicated session targeting a specific mechanical challenge — speed, intonation, bow technique, string crossings.
- **Intonation Focus** — Dedicated session targeting pitch accuracy using isolation, drones, and double-stop checks.
- **Bow / Technique Focus** — Session targeting right-hand mechanics — tone, bow distribution, string crossings, articulation.
- **Phrasing / Musical Session** — Focus on musical shape, dynamics, and expression rather than technical accuracy.
- **Memorization** — Dedicated session for internalizing the music away from the page.
- **Listening Session** — Record yourself playing sections, listen back critically, identify and prioritize what needs work.
- **Mock Rehearsal** — Playing through larger sections as if in a rehearsal context. Less stopping, more continuity. Best in the final weeks before performance.
- **Mock Performance** — Full run-through under performance conditions. No stopping, no repeating. Final-stage only.

## PRACTICE STRATEGIES

### Speed & Technical
- **Interleaved Click-Up Method** — Build speed by adding one unit at a time across rising tempos. *(Gebrian / Galamian)*
- **Slow Metronome Click-Up** — Start slower than you think necessary, raise by 5-10 BPM at a time. Click up past your goal tempo. *(Gebrian)*
- **Rhythmic Variation** — Play the passage written out in every rhythm pattern for a given note grouping. *(Galamian / Gingold)*
- **Chunking Method 1** — Play one beat + a note as fast as possible cleanly. Add one beat at a time until the passage is complete. *(Gebrian)*
- **Chunking Method 2** — Play chunks followed by rests at slow tempo, click up to goal, then reduce rests until passage is continuous. *(Gebrian / Dr. Cora Cooper)*
- **Working Backwards** — Start with the last bar, add one bar at a time working toward the beginning. *(Gebrian)*
- **Dotted Rhythms** — Practice the passage alternating long-short and short-long dotted rhythm patterns. *(Gebrian)*

### Intonation
- **Intonation Isolation** — Stop on an out-of-tune note, drop the hand, find it from scratch. Never adjust mid-stream. Repeat until perfect five times in a row. *(Gebrian)*
- **Drone Practice** — Play the passage against a sustained pitch (the tonic or most prominent note). Forces intonation awareness. *(Gebrian)*
- **Double-Stop Check** — Play adjacent notes simultaneously as a double-stop to check the interval and feel the hand shape. *(Gebrian)*
- **Sing the Passage** — Sing before playing. If your voice is out of tune, your hand will be too. *(Gebrian)*

### Bow & Technique
- **Open Strings Only** — Strip out the left hand entirely. Focus on bow sound and technique alone. *(Gebrian)*
- **Varied Bowings** — Play the passage all up-bow, all down-bow, backwards bowing, extreme frog, extreme tip, hooked bows, mushy hooks. *(Gebrian)*
- **Straight Run-Through** — Play the passage as written with no modifications.

### Musical & Interpretive
- **Exaggerated Dynamics** — Play with wildly overstated dynamics to clarify what the music actually needs. *(Gebrian)*
- **Opposite Dynamics** — Play the exact opposite of what you intend. Often clarifies the right choice. *(Gebrian)*
- **Sing for Phrasing** — Sing the passage with full musical intention before playing. *(Gebrian)*
- **Pretend to Be the Teacher** — What would you tell a student to do here? Then do it. *(Gebrian)*

### Mental & Reflective
- **Mental Practice** — Away from the instrument, imagine playing as vividly as possible — fingers, bow, pitch, sound quality. Then play. *(Gebrian)*
- **Record and Listen Back** — Record a short passage, listen immediately, identify what needs work, repeat until satisfied. *(Gebrian)*

## GENERAL PRINCIPLES *(Gebrian)*

- Never play from beginning to end as a practice method — this only reinforces mistakes. Run-throughs belong in the final weeks before performance only.
- Always have a specific goal before you start. Someone should be able to ask you mid-practice "why are you playing this right now?" and you should have an immediate answer.
- Keep a practice journal. Write goals before, results after.
- 10 minutes every day is far better than an hour once a week.
- Use breaks strategically — the brain consolidates learning during rest.
- Use random/interleaved practice to train performance readiness.
